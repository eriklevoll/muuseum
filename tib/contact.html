ཞལ་བྱང་།
The Estonian Old Technics Museum is located in Tallinn, Nõmme-Kase 12.
The museum can be visited all year round, however the time must be agreed upon prior to the visit. The museum is free of charge.

དམིགས་དོན།
The objective of the Old Technics Museum is to gather and store the early motoring history. Primary function of the museum is scientific research.

Current research topics:

The beginning of motoring history in the Baltic governorates
Activities and motorsport events of Pre World War I car clubs
Putting together a biographical lexicon of individuals associated with motoring in the Estonian governorate
Putting together a biographical lexicon of individuals associated with motoring in the Livonian governorate
Official state cars and machines of the imperial garage of the pre world war I era
ལོ་རྒྱུས་
1976 – The old technics club Unic was founded under the AUTOM union.
1998 – The non profit organization Unic was registered
2005 – creation of non profit organization Old Technics Union
2006 – The first motoring exhibition in Palmse manor
2007 – Establishing the Estonian Old Technics Museum Foundation
2011 – A new exhibition was opened in the current museum location at Nõmme-Kase 12, Tallinn

The people and organizations behind the foundation are mainly those who are associated with the club Unic.

The Estonian Old Technics Museum Foundation members are:
Chairman of the council – Peeter Mauer
Council members – Märt Karu, Heikki Perli, Tõnis Pappel, Urmas Teearu
Executive – Rene Levoll
གཅེས་བསྒྲིགས།
The theme of the exhibibition concentrates mainly on older russian/soviet technology.
The collection includes many exhibits with great historic value. Most notable are the ZIS 110 limousine used by Lavrentiy Beria and the Gaz 13 (Chaika) that the Finnish president Urho Kaleva Kekko used during his 1964 visit to Estonia.
